🧠 The Math Behind the Model

The linear regression trendline is calculated using this business formula:

Y = Target Outcome
 = 
(Slope × X) + Intercept
  • MSE (Forecast Error): Why approach 0?
    MSE mathematically squares every prediction error (the red lines on the chart). When MSE approaches 0, it means your trendline is touching every single data point perfectly—zero forecast error! If the number is huge, your model is practically guessing.
  • R² (Significance Score): Why 100%?
    If R² = 100%, it means 100% of the movement in your Goal (Y) is perfectly predicted by your Driver (X). If R² = 0%, there is no mathematical relationship at all. Aim for >80% to prove significance.
